Business Law

Business Law governs commercial activities, ensuring fair practices and protecting stakeholders. It provides the legal framework for businesses, covering contracts, corporate law, and intellectual property. Business Law facilitates commerce by establishing rules for transactions and resolving disputes.

Criminal Law

Criminal law governs actions harmful to society, defining offenses and prescribing punishments. It deals with conduct that is considered harmful to society, and prescribes punishment for such conduct. Criminal law defines crimes, sets out the procedures for prosecuting offenders, and establishes punishments.

Child Support

Child support is a legal obligation for parents to financially contribute to the upbringing of their children. It involves court-ordered payments to cover a child's basic needs, such as food, clothing, and housing. Child support ensures that children receive the financial support they need from both parents.

Education Law

Education law governs the legal framework for schools, students, and educators. It outlines rights, responsibilities, and regulations within educational institutions. Education law ensures equal access to education and maintains educational standards.

Divorce Law

Divorce law governs the legal dissolution of a marriage. It addresses issues like child custody, property division, and spousal support. Divorce law provides a legal framework for ending a marriage.

Tax Law

Tax law governs the collection of taxes, determining who owes taxes and how much. It outlines the rules and regulations for taxation of individuals and businesses. Tax law ensures that individuals and businesses pay their fair share to the government.
